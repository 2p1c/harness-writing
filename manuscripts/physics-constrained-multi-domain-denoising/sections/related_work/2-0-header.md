Laser ultrasonic testing offers non-contact inspection but faces inherent low signal-to-noise ratio, typically addressed through repeated signal averaging. This section reviews prior approaches across five areas: conventional signal processing methods for laser US denoising; deep learning architectures applied to ultrasonic signal enhancement; multi-domain fusion strategies that combine time, frequency, and time-frequency representations; physics-informed and physics-constrained neural networks that embed physical priors; and conventional baselines including Wiener filtering, BM3D, and wavelet denoising. Each category is examined to identify their respective capabilities and limitations in preserving acoustically relevant features while suppressing noise, establishing the foundation for the multi-domain physics-constrained approach proposed in this work.
