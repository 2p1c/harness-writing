\subsection{Key Findings}
\label{sec:conclusion-findings}

PMDF-Net achieves a peak SNR improvement of \SI{11.4}{dB} while preserving diagnostic signal features with higher fidelity than competing methods. This performance translates directly to practical workflow gains: a \num{16}$\times$ reduction in signal averaging (16 versus 256 averages) enables sub-surface defect detection in acquisition times compatible with industrial inspection throughput requirements. Critically, models trained exclusively on intact-specimen data generalize to defect-bearing testpieces without task-specific fine-tuning, demonstrating robust zero-shot transfer. Ablation studies confirm that both the multi-domain fusion and physics-constraint branches are individually insufficient — removing either component degrades SNR by more than \SI{3}{dB}. These results establish PMDF-Net as a viable pathway to practical laser ultrasonic inspection free from surface coupling requirements and the throughput bottlenecks of conventional averaging-based methods.
