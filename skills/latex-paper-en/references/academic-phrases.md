# Academic Phrase Corpus

High-quality academic expressions organized by section and function. Use these as inspiration; avoid mechanical application.

---

## Introduction Phrases

### Establishing Context
- "In recent years, [topic] has gained increasing attention from the research community due to its significant applications in [domain]."
- "Over the past decade, substantial progress has been made in understanding [phenomenon], yet challenges remain in [specific area]."
- "[Topic] has emerged as a fundamental building block in [larger field], enabling breakthroughs in [application area]."
- "The rapid advancement of [technology] has created unprecedented opportunities for [application], attracting considerable research interest."

### Identifying Gaps
- "Despite significant advances in [area], the problem of [specific challenge] remains largely unaddressed."
- "While prior work has explored [topic], there is a lack of systematic investigation into [understudied aspect]."
- "Existing approaches in [area] suffer from [limitation], which fundamentally constrains their applicability in [scenario]."
- "A critical gap exists between the theoretical potential of [method] and its practical performance in [real-world setting]."

### Stating Contributions
- "The key contributions of this paper are threefold:"
- "This paper makes the following original contributions:"
- "Unlike previous work, this paper presents the first comprehensive study of [topic]."
- "The proposed approach differs fundamentally from prior methods by [novel mechanism]."

### Outlining Structure
- "The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Section 2 describes..."
- "The paper is structured as follows. We first present... in Section 2. Section 3..."
- "The rest of this paper proceeds as follows. Section 2 introduces..."

## Methodology Phrases

### Describing Data Collection
- "The dataset consists of [description], comprising [number] samples collected from [source]."
- "Data were collected over a period of [timeframe] from [participants/sources]."
- "The sample was stratified according to [criterion] to ensure representativeness."
- "Participants were recruited using [method] and met the following criteria: [criteria]."

### Explaining Methods
- "We employ [method/algorithm] as the primary analytical tool, which enables [capability]."
- "The proposed approach builds upon [foundation] by incorporating [modification]."
- "To validate our hypothesis, we conducted [experiment type] following the protocol described in [reference]."
- "The implementation follows the standard procedure outlined in [reference], with [specific adaptation]."

### Reporting Parameters
- "Parameter settings follow the recommendations of [reference]: learning rate = [value], batch size = [value]."
- "All experiments were conducted using [hardware/software] with [configuration]."
- "We use [metric] as the primary evaluation criterion, consistent with prior work [cite]."

### Ensuring Rigor
- "To ensure the validity of our results, we conducted [additional validation method]."
- "The experimental setup follows established guidelines [cite] to facilitate comparison with prior work."
- "Statistical significance was assessed using [test] at the [α] significance level."

## Results Phrases

### Presenting Findings
- "The results demonstrate that [main finding], with [quantitative evidence]."
- "As shown in Figure/Table [ref], [variable 1] exhibits [behavior], while [variable 2] shows [behavior]."
- "The proposed method achieves [performance] on [dataset], outperforming the baseline by [margin]."
- "Results indicate a significant [relationship] between [variables] (p < [value], [test used])."

### Highlighting Statistics
- "The mean [metric] was [value] (SD = [value]), compared to [value] for the control group."
- "A [test] revealed a significant [effect] (F/[t]/[χ²] = [value], p < [value], η² = [value])."
- "The correlation between [var1] and [var2] was [direction] and statistically significant (r = [value], p < [value])."
- "Effect size analysis yielded [metric] = [value], indicating [interpretation]."

### Comparing with Baselines
- "Compared to [baseline method], the proposed approach achieves [X]% improvement in [metric]."
- "The performance gain over [baseline] is [quantified], demonstrating the effectiveness of [mechanism]."
- "While [method A] excels at [aspect], it struggles with [limitation]. In contrast, our approach..."

### Reporting Non-Significant Results
- "No significant difference was observed between [conditions] (p > .05)."
- "The effect of [variable] on [outcome] was not statistically significant (p = [value])."
- "Although [observation], this did not reach statistical significance ([value] > .05)."

## Discussion Phrases

### Interpreting Results
- "These findings suggest that [interpretation], indicating [broader meaning]."
- "A possible explanation for [finding] is that [mechanism], which is consistent with [theory/reference]."
- "The observed [phenomenon] can be attributed to [factor], as evidenced by [data/trend]."

### Comparing with Prior Work
- "This finding aligns with [cite] who reported similar [result] in [context]."
- "In contrast to [cite], who found [finding], our results demonstrate [different finding]."
- "While [cite] attributed [phenomenon] to [explanation], our analysis suggests [alternative explanation]."

### Discussing Implications
- "These results have important implications for [domain/practice]."
- "The findings contribute to [field] by demonstrating [theoretical contribution]."
- "Practitioners should consider [recommendation] when applying [method] in [setting]."

### Acknowledging Limitations
- "Several limitations should be acknowledged. First, [limitation 1] may affect [aspect]."
- "The generalizability of these findings is limited by [factor], which should be considered when [applying/interpretating]."
- "Although we [mitigation], [remaining limitation] remains a potential concern."

### Future Work
- "Future research could extend this work by investigating [direction]."
- "An open question remains: [question]. Addressing this would require [approach]."
- "The framework presented here provides a foundation for [extension], which we leave to future work."

## Conclusion Phrases

### Summarizing
- "In this paper, we presented [summary of work]."
- "This paper investigated [research question] and demonstrated [main finding]."
- "We have shown that [key result] through [evidence]."

### Emphasizing Contributions
- "The key contributions of this work include: (1) [contribution 1], (2) [contribution 2], and (3) [contribution 3]."
- "This work advances [field] by providing [theoretical/practical contribution]."
- "The proposed [method/framework] offers [benefits] for [stakeholders]."

### Closing
- "These findings underscore the importance of [insight] and open new avenues for [future direction]."
- "We believe this work represents a significant step toward [goal] and hope it will inspire further research in [area]."

---

## Hedging Phrases

### Softening Claims
- "The results suggest that..." (not "prove")
- "These findings indicate..." (not "demonstrate conclusively")
- "It appears that..." (not "it is certain that")
- "This may be due to..." (not "this is caused by")

### Expressing Uncertainty
- "Further investigation is needed to determine..."
- "It remains unclear whether..."
- "The extent to which... requires additional study."

### Conditional Statements
- "If [condition], then [prediction] would be expected."
- "These results hold under the assumption that [condition]."

- "As a result..."
- "Therefore..."

### Contrast
- "However..."
- "In contrast..."
- "On the other hand..."
- "Nevertheless..."

### Addition
- "Furthermore..."
- "Moreover..."
- "Additionally..."
- "In addition..."

### Exemplification
- "For instance..."
- "For example..."
- "Such as..."
- "As illustrated in..."

### Summary
- "In summary..."
- "To summarize..."
- "In conclusion..."
- "Overall..."
